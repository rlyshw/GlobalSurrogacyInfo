What is surrogacy?

Surrogacy is when a woman capable of bearing a child will lend their womb to another person. The surrogate mother will willingly become pregnant, bring a child to term, and deliver the child for someone else.

A surrogate mother can choose to become a surrogate for different reasons. An altruistic surrogate may choose to develop and deliver a baby for someone else as a gift. A commercial surrogate may be seeking monetary compensation for the user of their womb, and the service of child-bearing.

The developments of In-Vitro fertilization have made it possible for a surrogate mother to bear and deliver a child that is the genetic children of different parents. A surrogate client can have their reproductive cells implanted inside of the womb of a surrogate mother, and have their 100% genetic-match child born by a different woman. In-vitro fertilization has increased the demand for surrogacy around the globe.

Why?

Many Americans outsource their pregnancy over-sees because it "avoids the legal red tape and ill-defined surrogacy laws women face in the U.S"[5]. India in particular was a hot-bed for international surrogacy because of the relatively low cost and the fact that many doctors in India can speak English.

The low price of surrogacy in India has been criticized by international groups.
